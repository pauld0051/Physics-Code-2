Final Temperature Calculator for Mixtures

Calculate the final temperature of a mixture of two liquids given their masses, specific heat capacities, and initial temperatures.

Start by selecting the known variables below and entering their values.

Equation

\[ T_{f} = \frac{m_1 c_1 T_{i1} + m_2 c_2 T_{i2}}{m_1 c_1 + m_2 c_2}\]

Select the known variables:

Please select the known variables from the options below and enter their values to calculate the final temperature of a mixture of two liquids. You can select any two variables to calculate the third. This calculator assumes that the two liquids are well-mixed and that there is no heat loss to the surroundings.

The above equation is used to calculate the final temperature of the mixture with:

  • \(T_f\) is the final temperature of the mixture
  • \(m_1\) and \(m_2\) are the masses of the two liquids
  • \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) are the specific heat capacities of the two liquids
  • \(T_{i1}\) and \(T_{i2}\) are the initial temperatures of the two liquids
